The first declension is primarily composed of feminine nouns with very few exceptions to this. The declension is highly regular and is characterized by the presense of the vowel "a"

The genitive form of the first declension is -ae which is also repeated in the dative singular and nominative plural. The declension also has a long "a" in the ablative to distinguish from the nominative

To see the endings of all cases see the chart on the right.

The second declension is primarily composed of masculine and neuter nouns with very few exceptions to this. The declension is fairly regular and is characterized by the presense of the vowels "u" and "i"

The genitive form of the second declension masculine and neuter is -i. Neuter second declension nouns will have matching nominative and accusative forms. Masculine second declension nouns will have matching dative singular and ablative forms

To see the endings of all cases see the chart on the right.

The third declension is composed of masculine, feminine, and neuter nouns. This declension varies significantly in its forms depending upon the gender and identity of the noun.

The genitive form of the third declension is -is. Similar to the second declension, all neuter nouns will match in nominative and accusative forms. Nominative forms are mostly unpredictable in the third declension

To see the endings of all cases see the chart on the right.

The fourth declension is primarily composed of masculine and neuter nouns with very few exceptions to this. The declension is somewhat regular and is characterized by the presense of the vowel "u"

The genitive form of the fourth declension is -ūs which is also repeated in the accusative plural and nominative plural. The neuter fourth declension is almost entirely composed of -ū

To see the endings of all cases see the chart on the right.

The fifth declension is primarily composed of feminine nouns with very few exceptions to this. The declension is very regular and is characterized by the presense of the vowels "e" and "i"

The genitive form of the fourth declension is -ei which is also repeated in the dative singular. The fifth declension nominative plural also matches the accusative plural

To see the endings of all cases see the chart on the right.
